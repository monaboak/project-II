Wildfires

Los Angeles is suffers from natural forest fires. These fires go two ways- they run their course and burn out within a matter of hours, or they take on a life of their own and engulf dozens of cities in the Southern Californian region.

The earliest recorded wildfire in Los Angeles was the Bel Air/Brentwood fires, in early November of 1961. Wildfires can result from a combination of factors. The most common are fire-happy shrubs and trees, fierce autumn winds, long droughts ended by heavy rainfall, etc. Sometimes, wildfires are man made- meaning they result from innocent accidents and end up getting out of control. An example of this occurred in late 2019, when a couple attempted to do a gender reveal in a forest North of L.A. and ended up catching a bush on fire- which ended up causing mass destruction to a majority of Los Angeles county.
