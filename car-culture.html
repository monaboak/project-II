The wide mapped layout of the county, dense population, and high traffic is what contributes to the Los Angeles car culture being dangerous. Cars sitting in traffic causes a high level of car emission, which then produces a great deal of smog and eventually creates a hole in the ozone layer.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Why is smog dangerous?

Smog is defined as a haze combined with smoke and other pollutants in the atmosphere. Smog is considered to be dangerous because when inhaled, it irritates living organisms’ airways and can increase our risk of serious lung and heart diseases.

What contributes to the traffic in L.A.?

Essentially, the main driving force of L.A.’s notorious traffic is poor urban planning. The county’s population density is increasing, and the space remains the same so it’s getting gradually more crowded over a short amount of time.
